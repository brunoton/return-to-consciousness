# Against Asymmetric Methodological Restraint:

## A Meta-Argument for Consciousness-First Ontology

---

## 1. Shared First Principles (Minimal and Non-Controversial)

The argument proceeds from first principles that are widely accepted in contemporary analytic philosophy and explicitly endorsed by proponents of restraint-oriented realism:

**P1. Realism**
There is a mind-independent reality that inquiry aims to describe.

**P2. Fallibilism**
Our access to reality is imperfect, revisable, and non-absolute, but not arbitrary.

**P3. No View from Nowhere**
All evidence—empirical, mathematical, theoretical—is mediated by cognitive perspective.

**P4. Explanatory Coherence**
Ceteris paribus, theories that unify domains and reduce explanatory fragmentation are epistemically preferable.

**P5. Bruteness Is an Epistemic Cost**
Brute facts may be unavoidable, but minimizing them is rational.

These principles are deliberately minimal. Rejecting any of them incurs substantial independent philosophical cost.

---

## 2. The Competing Meta-Epistemic Stances

### Asymmetric Methodological Restraint (AMR)

> When ontological frameworks are underdetermined by third-person evidence, ontological commitment should be suspended—*especially* when revision is motivated by phenomenological or consciousness-based considerations.

Crucially, restraint is applied **asymmetrically**:

* Ontological commitments grounded in third-person structure (e.g. modality, abstraction, lawhood) are permitted under underdetermination.
* Ontological commitments grounded in phenomenology or consciousness are not.

### Consciousness-First Ontology (CFO)

> When a metaphysical framework systematically dissolves persistent explanatory failures across multiple domains—including consciousness, physics, and normativity—ontological revision is warranted, even under underdetermination.

The disagreement is **meta-epistemic**, not empirical.

---

## 3. Core Argument

### Premise 1

Underdetermination is endemic in foundational inquiry (quantum mechanics, modality, consciousness, cosmology).

*Accepted by both sides.*

### Premise 2

AMR treats underdetermination as a **blocking condition** on ontological revision when such revision elevates consciousness to fundamental status.

*By definition of AMR.*

### Premise 3

Proponents of AMR nevertheless accept ontological commitments under underdetermination in other domains (e.g. objective modality, necessary truths, abstract structure).

*Established by philosophical practice.*

### Premise 4

Therefore, restraint under AMR is **selective**, not general.

*From Premises 2 and 3.*

### Premise 5

Selective restraint requires a principled criterion distinguishing permissible from impermissible ontological revision.

*Logical requirement.*

### Premise 6

The operative criterion excludes phenomenological and first-person evidence from carrying ontological weight, while permitting third-person structural considerations to do so.

*De facto evidential asymmetry.*

### Premise 7

This asymmetry constitutes a substantive metaphysical constraint on what is permitted to be fundamental.

*From P3 and evidential symmetry.*

---

## 4. Consequence

Asymmetric Methodological Restraint is **not metaphysically neutral**.
It encodes a background ontology in which:

* consciousness is epistemically secondary,
* phenomenology is evidentially downgraded,
* explanatory unification across first- and third-person domains is disallowed,
* brute facts are preferred to ontological revision.

This violates **P4 (Explanatory Coherence)** and **P5 (Minimization of Brute Facts)**.

---

## 5. Comparative Evaluation

### Consciousness-First Ontology (CFO)

* Treats phenomenology and physics symmetrically (P3)
* Reduces brute facts (qualia, measurement, psychophysical laws)
* Explains why physicalist “neutrality” systematically generates anomalies
* Accepts ontological risk to achieve explanatory unification

### Asymmetric Methodological Restraint (AMR)

* Preserves underdetermination at the cost of fragmentation
* Tolerates indefinitely many brute facts
* Functions as de facto physicalism without explicit endorsement
* Blocks ontological revision even under sustained explanatory pressure

---

## 6. Conclusion

**Given shared first principles (P1–P5), Consciousness-First Ontology is more coherent than Asymmetric Methodological Restraint.**

AMR is internally consistent but epistemically suboptimal: it smuggles metaphysical constraints while disallowing the only ontological move that systematically dissolves the deepest explanatory failures.

The rational choice is therefore not certainty, but **ontological risk in pursuit of coherence**, rather than restraint that preserves fragmentation.

---

## One-Sentence Summary

> *If explanatory coherence matters and brute facts are epistemic costs, then applying restraint asymmetrically to exclude consciousness-first ontology is less rational than adopting it under sustained explanatory pressure.*
